Few, if any, industries have suffered as much as Major League Baseball over the last couple of years, especially since the start of the MLB season. Maybe it's a generational change or something to do with a time-sensitive sport like baseball that gives people some sort of time-sucking high, but the sights, sounds and effects of Major League Baseball don't exactly tug at our heartstrings. It isn't the No. 1 sport in the United States. It's not even the most popular one. (I repeat, it's not even the most popular one.) The falling television ratings, dwindling attendance and even a decided drop in fantasy leagues surrounding Major League Baseball all concern commissioner Rob Manfred, but what he's particularly worried about is the pandemic.
